What the symptom can indicate

01

Intermittent leaks can be driven by product contamination rather than average jaw temperature.

02

Displayed temperature may differ from actual jaw-face temperature or vary across the seal width.

03

A seal that looks acceptable immediately may weaken after cooling, handling or product exposure.

Buyer analysis

Classify the leak location

Separate longitudinal, cross-seal, corner, zipper and top-seal failures. Photograph the seal and retain failed samples so settings can be connected to a physical defect pattern.

Build a controlled seal window

Using final material, vary temperature, pressure and dwell one factor at a time. Inspect seal strength, distortion, product squeeze-out and cooling behavior rather than selecting the hottest acceptable appearance.

Reproduce contamination deliberately

Introduce the realistic powder, oil or product fragment load expected near the seal. This shows whether dosing control and settling time are adequate at production speed.

Symptom-to-action diagnostic matrix

Observed pattern Evidence to check Controlled next action
Straight channel through seal Product or wrinkle at channel, jaw flatness Remove contamination source and verify jaw contact
Seal peels with low force Material seal window, actual temperature, dwell Establish a validated operating window
Random corner leaks Pouch alignment, zipper transition, cooling Stabilize geometry and post-seal handling
