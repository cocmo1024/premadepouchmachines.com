What the symptom can indicate

01

Pouch stacks change friction as the magazine level falls or compressed zipper areas build uneven height.

02

Static and surface treatment can create double picks that sensors do not always distinguish.

03

A gripper jam may begin upstream when the pouch is handed off late or out of center.

Buyer analysis

Observe the complete handoff

Use slow motion video from magazine pickup through first gripper closure. Mark the first point where the pouch loses position instead of diagnosing from the final jam location.

Control magazine stack geometry

Check guide width, stack angle, pouch curl, zipper buildup and minimum magazine level. Reload practice should keep pressure and pouch orientation consistent.

Challenge missed and double-pick detection

Create controlled no-pouch and double-pouch events during testing. Verify the machine response, recovery sequence and prevention of product discharge without a valid pouch.

Symptom-to-action diagnostic matrix

Observed pattern Evidence to check Controlled next action
Missed pickup increases as stack gets low Magazine angle, follower pressure, pickup stroke Stabilize stack presentation across full range
Two pouches enter together Static, pouch blocking, separator condition Improve separation and validate double-pick response
Pouch enters gripper at an angle Transfer timing, guide clearance, gripper level Correct handoff before downstream stations
