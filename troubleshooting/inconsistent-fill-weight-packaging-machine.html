What the symptom can indicate

01

Average weight can remain on target while individual packs exceed tolerance.

02

Feeder refill can change head pressure, density or product distribution and create a repeating weight pattern.

03

Checkweigher readings may include package tare variation or vibration that is not caused by the filler.

Buyer analysis

Plot weights in production order

A time-ordered chart reveals refill cycles, warm-up drift, periodic vibration and changeover effects that disappear in a simple average and standard deviation.

Separate dosing variation from tare

Weigh empty packages and product independently where possible. Confirm the reference scale and checkweigher before changing filler settings.

Control product state during the test

Record density, temperature, moisture, particle distribution and supply level. A stable machine cannot compensate for uncontrolled product segregation or aeration.

Symptom-to-action diagnostic matrix

Observed pattern Evidence to check Controlled next action
Weight steps after hopper refill Density, head pressure, refill timing Stabilize refill and compensate only after measurement
Random wide variation Product flow, vibration, sticking, gate closure Remove intermittent flow and discharge faults
Filler and checkweigher disagree Tare, calibration, conveyor vibration Verify the measurement system first
