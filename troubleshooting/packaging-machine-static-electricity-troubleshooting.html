What the symptom can indicate

01

Static symptoms often rise in dry conditions and disappear before maintenance can reproduce them.

02

Grounding removes charge from conductive parts but does not neutralize an insulating film surface.

03

Ionizers can be ineffective when placed too far away, blocked by machine parts or poorly maintained.

Buyer analysis

Measure conditions with the failure

Record humidity, material lot, line speed and where cling or repulsion begins. Compare good and failed periods instead of assuming every feeding issue is static.

Control the charge at its source

Inspect high-friction film paths, separation points, plastic guides and poor grounding. Use ionization close to the affected surface where appropriate and maintain emitters according to their requirements.

Verify the result through reject data

Track double picks, missed counts, film wraps or opening failures before and after changes. Visual improvement without reject reduction is not a complete fix.

Symptom-to-action diagnostic matrix

Observed pattern Evidence to check Controlled next action
Pouches cling or double pick Humidity, separation point, surface charge Neutralize near magazine and improve separation
Film wraps around roller Grounding, friction point, roller cleanliness Remove charge source and contamination
Powder jumps to seal or film Charge location, airflow, humidity Combine dust and static controls

RFQ checklist for this page

A short video showing the complete machine cycle before, during and after the fault.

Photos of good and failed packs with the defect location marked.

Current recipe, setpoints, alarms, line speed and dynamic air or vacuum readings where relevant.

Product, pouch or film samples from both good and failed lots.

Recent change parts, maintenance actions, material changes and the measured reject rate.
