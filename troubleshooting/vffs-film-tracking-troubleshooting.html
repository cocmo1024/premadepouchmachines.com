What the symptom can indicate

01

A skewed roll or uneven unwind tension can create drift before the film reaches the forming collar.

02

Contaminated or uneven pull belts can steer the formed tube while appearing to be a collar problem.

03

Material gauge, stiffness and curl changes can alter tracking after a film-lot change.

Buyer analysis

Mark the first point of lateral movement

Run at controlled speed and observe roll, dancer, guide rollers, collar entry and pull belts. Correct the earliest deviation rather than shifting downstream parts to mask it.

Check mechanical references before recipes

Verify roll centering, roller cleanliness, parallelism, forming-set seating and equal pull-belt pressure. Save recipe changes only after the mechanical path is stable.

Compare film lots with measured properties

Record width, thickness, splice quality, curl and coefficient-of-friction changes. Retain good and failed samples for the material supplier and machine trial.

Symptom-to-action diagnostic matrix

Observed pattern Evidence to check Controlled next action
Web drifts before collar Roll centering, unwind brake, guide rollers Correct unwind geometry and tension
Tube shifts after forming Collar seating, pull belts, vertical seal drag Balance formed-tube traction
Problem appears with new film lot Width, gauge, curl, friction Validate material variation before retuning machine
