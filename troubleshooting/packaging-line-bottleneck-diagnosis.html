What the symptom can indicate

01

The slowest rated machine is not always the constraint when micro-stops and starvation dominate.

02

Short speed trials hide accumulation, refill, cleaning and reject-recovery losses.

03

Increasing one machine's speed can transfer jams and labor demand downstream.

Buyer analysis

Measure states, not only downtime totals

Record running, starved, blocked, faulted, changeover and planned-stop time for each process. A state timeline shows how one disturbance propagates through the line.

Use good output at the final boundary

Count accepted cases or finished packs after inspection, not primary-machine cycles. Include rejects, rework and unplanned manual intervention.

Test one constraint hypothesis at a time

Temporarily add buffer, stabilize feeding or reduce downstream demand and observe the line response. Improvement confirms the constraint before capital is committed.

Symptom-to-action diagnostic matrix

Observed pattern Evidence to check Controlled next action
Primary machine often starved Upstream feed state and buffer level Stabilize supply before increasing machine speed
Primary machine often blocked Inspection and downstream discharge Remove downstream constraint or add controlled accumulation
Rated speed reached only briefly Micro-stops, refill, cleaning, rejects Design around sustained good output

RFQ checklist for this page

A short video showing the complete machine cycle before, during and after the fault.

Photos of good and failed packs with the defect location marked.

Current recipe, setpoints, alarms, line speed and dynamic air or vacuum readings where relevant.

Product, pouch or film samples from both good and failed lots.

Recent change parts, maintenance actions, material changes and the measured reject rate.
