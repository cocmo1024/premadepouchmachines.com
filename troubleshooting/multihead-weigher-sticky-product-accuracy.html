What the symptom can indicate

01

Product left in a bucket becomes part of the next combination and creates unexplained overweights.

02

High vibration can improve feed rate while damaging product or separating blends.

03

Residue buildup changes friction during the shift and invalidates early settings.

Buyer analysis

Inspect release, not only target weight

Film feeder pans and bucket discharge. Confirm that every selected bucket empties fully and reaches the packaging machine within the expected timing window.

Tune distribution before combination logic

Center-cone loading, radial feeder balance and product depth must be stable before chasing software parameters. Uneven distribution limits the combinations available to the controller.

Include buildup in the test duration

Run long enough to reproduce oil, sugar or coating accumulation. Define cleaning triggers and include this planned downtime in capacity calculations.

Symptom-to-action diagnostic matrix

Observed pattern Evidence to check Controlled next action
Frequent overweight packs Bucket residue, delayed discharge Improve complete release before retuning target
No combination or slow cycles Pan distribution, product depth, sticky bridging Stabilize feed to all heads
Accuracy degrades over time Surface buildup, temperature, coating Define cleaning interval and surface controls
