What the symptom can indicate

01

A package can appear tight at discharge yet contain a seal channel that opens during handling.

02

Sharp product edges and trapped particles can create delayed punctures.

03

Warm product or natural gas release can relax the pack without an external leak.

Buyer analysis

Record when the package changes

Compare immediately after sealing, after cooling, after drop or compression handling and after storage. Timing narrows the likely failure mode.

Inspect seal and body separately

Use an agreed leak-test method, then localize failures to the seal, corners, folds or product-contact punctures. Retain the failed package before opening it.

Reproduce commercial handling

Case compression, conveyor drops and sharp product orientation can create faults absent from a stationary machine sample. Include downstream handling in acceptance.

Symptom-to-action diagnostic matrix

Observed pattern Evidence to check Controlled next action
Loose immediately after sealing Vacuum level, dwell, seal closure Correct evacuation and sealing sequence
Tight pack relaxes after handling Puncture, fold, corner abrasion Improve material and product orientation
Pack relaxes without detected leak Product temperature, outgassing, test sensitivity Evaluate product condition and method
