What the symptom can indicate

01

A stable central channel can form while powder remains stationary against the hopper wall.

02

Excessive agitation may compact powder, generate heat or change density instead of improving flow.

03

Refill events can collapse a bridge and create a sudden overfeed or weight shift.

Buyer analysis

Observe the hopper surface during dosing

Record whether the powder level descends evenly, forms a central funnel or stops until refill. Link each visible event to the time-ordered package weights.

Change geometry before adding force

Review hopper angle, agitator clearance, screw inlet and refill location. More agitation is not a universal solution and can alter density or damage product.

Test across realistic residence time

Moisture, vibration and compaction can worsen after the powder sits in the hopper. A duration trial is needed when short samples do not reproduce production stoppages.

Symptom-to-action diagnostic matrix

Observed pattern Evidence to check Controlled next action
Weight falls gradually Hopper funnel, screw inlet starvation Restore consistent powder supply to auger
Sudden heavy dose after refill Bridge collapse, density shift Control refill location and hopper flow
Flow worsens during long run Moisture, compaction, heat, buildup Test residence-time effects and cleanability
