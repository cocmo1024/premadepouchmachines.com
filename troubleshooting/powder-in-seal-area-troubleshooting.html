What the symptom can indicate

01

Powder can continue falling after auger stop because of product in the tube and airborne fines.

02

Bag vibration or product impact can throw material upward into the open seal area.

03

Dust extraction placed incorrectly can disturb the dose or draw film toward the nozzle.

Buyer analysis

Separate tailing from airborne dust

Use high-speed video and clean test film to identify whether contamination is a concentrated late stream or dispersed dust. The two patterns require different corrections.

Coordinate cut-off, settling and jaw closure

Auger stop, gate action, bag support, dust extraction and seal timing should be reviewed as one sequence. A short controlled delay may improve good output more than a faster nominal cycle.

Measure the result with package tests

Track contaminated-seal rate, seal strength and leak failures at the target speed. Visual cleanliness alone is not a complete acceptance criterion.

Symptom-to-action diagnostic matrix

Observed pattern Evidence to check Controlled next action
Dense streak of powder in one location Auger tailing, nozzle cut-off, product string Improve cut-off and tube clearance
Fine dust across entire seal Airflow, drop height, extraction position Contain airborne powder before jaw closure
Contamination rises with speed Settling time, bag support, jaw timing Optimize sequence around good packs per minute
