What the symptom can indicate

01

Zipper compression and lip offset vary between pouch suppliers and lots.

02

A partially open zipper limits nozzle entry and can cause product to strike the top seal area.

03

Heat or jaw pressure near the zipper transition can distort the pouch and create corner leaks.

Buyer analysis

Measure pouch opening force and usable mouth width

Test multiple pouches from each lot and record the minimum opening needed for the fill nozzle. A nominal pouch width does not describe the clearance available above the zipper.

Protect the zipper and top-seal zone during filling

Center the pouch, control nozzle depth and limit product bounce. The fill level and settling time should keep both zipper tracks and the final seal area clean.

Challenge the zipper transition corners

Inspect top-seal strength and leaks where the zipper profile ends. Validate jaw contact, temperature and pouch geometry with the final commercial material.

Symptom-to-action diagnostic matrix

Observed pattern Evidence to check Controlled next action
Pouch mouth opens but zipper remains closed Opening-force variation, opener position Use zipper-specific opening action and pouch control
Product lands above zipper Nozzle clearance, pouch opening, fill trajectory Center fill path and increase usable opening
Leaks near zipper ends Jaw contact, transition thickness, alignment Validate corner sealing with final pouch
