Why a premade pouch does not open and how to isolate the cause.

What the symptom can indicate

01

Pouch lips may be blocked by static, zipper compression, surface treatment or dimensional variation.

02

A pickup problem can look like an opening problem after the pouch reaches the station out of position.

03

Vacuum level at the pump does not prove vacuum flow and seal condition at the suction cup.

Buyer analysis

Start with a pouch-only challenge

Measure width, lip offset, zipper position and opening force across good and failed pouch lots. Hand-open samples and check whether the same face consistently resists separation.

Trace the vacuum path to the cup

Inspect cup wear, hose cracks, filters, valves and timing. Compare vacuum at the opening device during the actual cycle rather than relying only on the supply gauge.

Change one timing boundary at a time

Confirm pickup position, gripper centering and opening dwell before changing fill timing. Record the baseline and reject rate after each controlled adjustment.

Symptom-to-action diagnostic matrix

Observed pattern Evidence to check Controlled next action
Pouch reaches station but lips stay closed Opening force, cup contact, vacuum at cup Separate pouch-lot resistance from suction loss
One side opens and the other remains closed Pouch centering, cup alignment, gripper level Correct position before increasing vacuum
Pouch opens then collapses Opening dwell, air assist, transfer timing Stabilize the open state before filling
