Buyer analysis

Machine risks created by recyclable structures

Mono-material and downgauged structures can behave differently during forming, pouch pickup, filling, heat sealing and discharge. VFFS projects should test film tracking and forming stability. Premade pouch projects should test pouch opening, zipper behavior and seal strength. Sachet and stick-pack projects should confirm cut quality, tear notch and easy-open performance.

How to write the RFQ

State the current material, the planned recyclable structure, the target launch market and the required pack tests. Ask for a sealing-window test and sample run instead of accepting a generic statement that recyclable films are supported.

Sources and standards

European Commission PPWR context

The EU Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation entered force in 2025 and targets economically viable recyclability by 2030.

Common decision questions

Does PPWR require a different packaging machine?

Not always. The machine frame may stay similar, but forming parts, sealing jaws, temperature control, film handling and acceptance testing may need adjustment.

Can mono-material pouches run on a premade pouch machine?

Often yes, but pouch opening, stiffness, zipper performance and seal strength must be tested with real pouch samples.

What should buyers test first?

Start with sealing window, leak performance, pack appearance, forming stability and output at the target speed.
