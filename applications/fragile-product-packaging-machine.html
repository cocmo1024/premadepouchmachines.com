What the project must solve

01

Breakage added by elevators, weighers and transfer chutes can exceed damage created inside the bagger.

02

Faster drops may improve cycles while reducing saleable yield and changing the apparent fill volume.

03

Finished cases can damage packs that passed primary-machine inspection.

Buyer analysis

Establish a baseline before the line trial

Measure existing broken-product percentage at the machine inlet. After the trial, separate damage caused by feeding, dosing, primary packing and secondary handling so corrective work targets the right stage.

Shorten and soften every transfer

Low drop heights, controlled chute angles, suitable bucket discharge and restrained conveyor gaps reduce impacts. For regular products, horizontal flow wrapping can avoid the free fall used by vertical baggers.

Buy saleable yield, not only cycles per minute

The acceptance plan should define breakage, package appearance, seal integrity and weight accuracy at the required output. Report good packs per hour after case packing.
