What the project must solve

01

Product can bridge on feeder pans or remain in buckets, creating slow cycles and weight inconsistency.

02

Oil, sugar or coating can transfer to contact surfaces and change friction during a production shift.

03

Aggressive vibration can separate blends or damage product while attempting to improve flow.

Buyer analysis

Test the commercial surface condition

Send samples with the same oil, sugar, seasoning, temperature and storage age seen in production. Fresh laboratory samples may flow much better than normal factory material.

Balance release against product care

Contact finish, feeder geometry, bucket timing and controlled vibration should be tuned together. A stable result releases the full dose without bruising product or separating a blend.

Include cleaning frequency in output calculations

If residue progressively changes flow, planned wipe-down or wash intervals are part of capacity. Acceptance should include a duration run long enough to reveal buildup.
