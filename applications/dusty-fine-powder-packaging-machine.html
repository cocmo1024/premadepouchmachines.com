What the project must solve

01

Aerated bulk density can change after conveying, so an auger setting that is stable at the hopper may drift after refill.

02

Airborne powder on the seal face can create channels, weak seals and a misleading gap between machine speed and good bags per minute.

03

Dust collection, electrical classification and operator exposure must be assessed for the actual material rather than assumed from a food-grade frame.

Buyer analysis

Measure settled and aerated density

Send both values, particle-size information and a sample from normal production. Feeder design, hopper agitation, auger geometry and refill logic should be tested with the powder state the machine will actually receive.

Treat the seal area as a process boundary

Dust extraction near dosing, controlled product fall, bag settling time and seal-face cleaning can matter more than nominal cycle speed. Acceptance should count saleable bags after leak and seal inspection.

Do not infer dust safety from stainless steel

Food-contact construction does not establish combustible-dust suitability. The plant must complete its own material hazard and area review before electrical, extraction and housekeeping requirements are finalized.
