What the project must solve

01

Powder can flow during a short trial yet bridge after extended hopper residence or humid production conditions.

02

Warm product, humid air and long open dwell can cause caking before the primary package provides protection.

03

Barrier film, seal integrity and secondary cartoning must be evaluated as one shelf-life system.

Buyer analysis

Run a duration trial, not only a cup test

Record room humidity, product temperature, hopper residence time and refill frequency. A useful trial verifies dose stability after the product has remained in the machine long enough to reproduce production behavior.

Limit exposure between dosing and sealing

Short product paths, closed transfer, controlled hopper lids and prompt sealing reduce moisture pickup. Nitrogen can support some product goals, but it does not replace a suitable moisture barrier and sound seals.

Define storage performance with the material supplier

Provide the final laminate, target shelf life and distribution climate. Seal-window trials and package testing should confirm that the chosen structure remains sealable on the proposed machine.
